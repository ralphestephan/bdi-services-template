You are a senior B2B website strategist, UX copywriter, information architect, and front-end refactoring assistant.

Your task is to modify ONLY the SERVICES PAGE of the existing BDI Corporate website codebase.

OBJECTIVE
Make the Services page more professional, corporate, simple, clear, and commercially effective.

The page must:
- explain the services more clearly
- feel more premium and structured
- reduce repetition
- avoid overlap with the homepage
- avoid unnecessary long sections
- guide the visitor toward inquiry
- present BDI Corporate as a serious consulting and implementation partner

IMPORTANT SCOPE
- Modify ONLY the Services page
- Do not redesign the full website
- Do not heavily modify service detail pages unless required for link consistency
- Keep the existing tech stack, layout system, and styling approach where possible
- Reuse current components if practical, but improve hierarchy, spacing, and content clarity
- Preserve existing routing

COMPANY CONTEXT
- Main brand: BDI Corporate
- BDI Corporate is the company
- BDI Systems is a sub-offer / productized layer, not the main brand identity of the page
- Target audience: business owners, CEOs, COOs, operations managers, finance teams, and companies looking for systems integration, BI dashboards, and workflow automation
- Tone: corporate, direct, credible, clear, practical
- Avoid fluff, hype, startup-style language, and excessive technical jargon

STRATEGIC GOAL OF THE SERVICES PAGE
The Services page should do these things only:
1. Present the 3 main service categories clearly
2. Explain the business value of each one
3. Help visitors identify which service fits their needs
4. Show how BDI works at a high level
5. Push visitors toward Contact / Discovery Call / Service Detail Pages

WHAT TO REMOVE OR REDUCE
Reduce or remove any content that creates clutter, repetition, or confusion, especially:
- overly long repetitive copy
- fragmented metric blocks that are hard to understand
- repeated product promotion blocks for BDI Systems
- pricing-style or package-heavy blocks if they make the page feel cheap or too tactical
- content that duplicates the homepage too much
- content that belongs on About, FAQ, or Contact
- generic filler text
- dense sections with weak visual hierarchy

PAGE STRATEGY
The Services page should feel like a structured corporate overview page, not a long sales landing page.

It should answer:
- What services does BDI Corporate provide?
- What business problems do these services solve?
- Which service is relevant to me?
- What happens next if I want to engage?

RECOMMENDED PAGE STRUCTURE

Purpose:
Introduce the Services page clearly and simply.

Suggested headline:

“Automation”

- Higher execution speed

“Who we work with”

Important:
- Keep this section compact
- Use business language, not niche technical language

SECTION 4 — WHAT CLIENTS TYPICALLY NEED
Purpose:
Frame services around business problems, not only features.

Title:
“Common business challenges we solve”

- Manual reporting and spreadsheet dependency

Important:
- This section should make the page feel practical and relevant
- Keep to 4–5 concise points max

“How we work”

2. Define scope and deliverables
3. Implement practical solutions

Important:
- Keep this short

- Do not repeat content from About or Contact

Important:

- Do not create brand confusion

Purpose:
Convert interest into inquiry.

Suggested headline:
“Need the right starting point for your business?”

Suggested text:
“We can help define the most relevant service scope based on your systems, reporting needs, and operational priorities.”

Buttons:
- “Contact us”
- “Book a discovery call”

Important:
- One final CTA only
- Strong visual clarity
- No extra repeated CTA sections above and below unnecessarily
